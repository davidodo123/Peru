The Great Inca Legacy

The Inca Empire, known as Tawantinsuyu, was the largest civilization in pre-Columbian America. Rising from the heart of Cusco, they masterfully conquered the rugged Andean landscape, building architectural wonders that still stand today.

From the sacred valley to the clouds of Machu Picchu, the Inca legacy is not just in the stones, but in the living traditions, languages, and agricultural techniques that continue to define the Peruvian spirit.

TOUR GUIDES

Machu Picchu: The Incan Masterpiece

Recognized as one of the New Seven Wonders of the World, this 15th-century citadel sits high in the Andes mountains. It remains the most iconic symbol of the Inca Empire's architectural genius and spiritual connection to the cosmos

5 - 7 Days Inca Trail

A 15th-century citadel built by Emperor Pachacuti

Constructed with the Ashlar technique—stones cut to fit together without mortar.

Reach the summit via the classic 4-day Inca Trail for the ultimate.

Cusco: The Heart of the Empire

The historic capital of the Incas is a living museum. Walk through streets where ancient stone walls support colonial mansions, and explore the vibrant Plaza de Armas, once the center of the largest civilization in the pre-Columbian Americas.

3 - 4 Days Boat & Canopy walks

Visit San Pedro Market to taste traditional Andean grains.

Home to the Qorikancha

Once the capital of the Tawantinsuyu Empire

Arequipa: The White City

Surrounded by three volcanoes, Arequipa is famous for its stunning architecture built from white volcanic stone (sillar). Its historic center is a UNESCO World Heritage site, offering a perfect blend of Spanish heritage and Andean spirit.

5 - 7 Days Inca Trail

Built almost entirely from sillar, a white volcanic stone from the Misti volcano

A "city within a city" featuring vibrant blue and red colonial architecture

Colca Canyon: Flight of the Condor

One of the deepest canyons in the world, the Colca Valley is a land of dramatic landscapes and ancient traditions. It is the best place in South America to witness the majestic flight of the Andean Condor over terraced green mountains.

3 - 4 Days Boat & Canopy walks

One of the world's deepest canyons, reaching depths of 3,270 meters

The best location in the Andes to see the giant Andean Condor in its natural habitat
